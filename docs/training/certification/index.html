Certification Levels

The Provo Auxiliary Communications Team (PACT) certification program is designed to prepare members for emergency operations through structured training and real-world participation.

Participation is not required, but it is strongly encouraged.

As members progress through certification levels, they develop increased confidence, technical proficiency, and familiarity with communication equipment and procedures.

CERTIFICATION LEVELS

★ Tip: Each level builds on the previous one and increases your readiness and responsibility within PACT.

How Do I Get Credit?​

After completing all requirements for a certification level, email the PACT Membership Coordinator for verification. Once approved, your certification will be recorded and you will receive a signed certificate at the next quarterly interface meeting.

Members are expected to maintain the equipment required for each certification level they have completed.

While participation with other organizations is encouraged, only officially sponsored PACT activities count toward certification requirements. A list of qualifying events can be found on the Training Exercises & Events page.

PACT’s certification program is similar to the UCARES certification requirements for those pursuing both programs.

Equipment Requirements​

Radio Equipment

  • 1.3 2m/70cm handheld radio

    • Spare battery for handheld radio
    • Earpiece for handheld radio
    • Upgraded handheld radio antenna (whip antenna)
    • Handheld radio programmed with PACT Standard Load

  • 1.4 Maintain a documentation binder

    • PACT Field Manual
    • Provo street map
    • PACT district & frequency map
    • Notebook
    • Zipper pouch with writing utensils
    • Radio manual for handheld radio

Essentials

  • 1.5 Essentials

    • Multitool or Pocketknife
    • Reusable water bottle
    • Personal First Aid Kit

Optional Items

  • 1.6 Purchase PACT Apparel

    • PACT Ballcap
    • PACT T-shirt

Optional: Not required for certification

Participation Requirements​

Participation

  • 1.7 Participate in three Weekly Training Nets

LEVEL 2​

Download Level 2 Checklist

Prerequisites​

Prerequisites

  • 2.1 Complete the Level 1 Certification

Equipment Requirements​

Portable Antenna

  • 2.2 Portable 2m/70cm antenna (j-pole, ground plane, etc)
  • 2.3 50' coax with PL259 connectors (RG-8 or better)
  • 2.4 One roll of electrical tape

Coaxial Adapters

  • 2.5 Coaxial Adapters

    • SO239 - SO239
    • SO239 - PL259
    • SO239 - BNC Male
    • SO239 - BNC Female
    • SO239 - SMA Male Pigtail
    • SO239 - SMA Female Pigtail
    • PL259 - BNC Male
    • PL259 - BNC Female
    • Any other specific adaptors your radio needs to connect to coax with PL259 connectors

USB Cables

  • 2.6 USB Cables

    • USB A - Micro USB
    • USB A - USB C
    • USB C - USB C

Training Requirements​

Training Requirements

  • 2.7 ICS-100.C
  • 2.8 ICS-200.C

Participation Requirements​

Participation

  • 2.9 Participate in five Weekly Training Nets (must be different than the ones used for a previous certification level)
  • 2.10 Attend one Quarterly Interface

Download Level 3 Checklist

Prerequisites​

Prerequisites

  • 3.1 Complete the Level Two Certification

Equipment Requirements​

Anderson Power Poles

  • 3.2 Anderson Power Poles

    • Power pole to cigarette lighter
    • Power pole to alligator clip
    • Power pole to extension cable

Training Requirements

  • 3.3 ICS-700.B
